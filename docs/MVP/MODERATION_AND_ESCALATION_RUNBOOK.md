# Dorea Network — MVP Moderation & Escalation Runbook

## Purpose

This document defines the operational moderation and escalation procedures for the Dorea MVP pilot.

The purpose of this runbook is to provide:
- lightweight operational guidance
- clear escalation expectations
- safety visibility
- leadership accountability
- continuity support

The MVP moderation model is intentionally:
- human-centered
- lightweight
- operationally practical
- non-bureaucratic

The MVP is NOT intended to function as:
- a crisis response platform
- a law enforcement system
- a clinical counseling system
- a highly automated moderation engine

---

# Core Principle

> No one is left alone.

Moderation and escalation exist to:
- protect people
- improve visibility
- support continuity
- enable timely human response

NOT:
- punish users
- optimize engagement
- automate ministry decisions
- create surveillance systems

---

# Human-Centered Rule

All moderation and escalation decisions require human oversight.

AI may assist with:
- visibility
- reminders
- routing
- queue prioritization

AI must NOT:
- autonomously intervene
- diagnose mental health conditions
- replace pastoral discernment
- make final escalation decisions
- impersonate trusted relationships

---

# MVP Escalation Philosophy

The escalation system exists to:
- surface urgent concerns
- notify responsible leaders
- preserve continuity
- support timely human action

The MVP should prioritize:
- clarity
- simplicity
- rapid visibility
- limited operational burden

---

# Escalation Severity Levels

## Severity 1 — Critical

Examples:
- active self-harm threat
- suicide threat
- abuse disclosure
- immediate safety threat
- violent threat

### Required Response

```text
Immediate human review required
```

### Recommended Actions

- notify designated leaders immediately
- contact emergency services if appropriate
- escalate to pastoral leadership
- document actions taken
- restrict visibility appropriately

---

## Severity 2 — High

Examples:
- severe emotional crisis
- repeated unresolved distress
- harassment reports
- concerning behavioral escalation

### Required Response

```text
Review within 1 hour
```

### Recommended Actions

- assign responsible leader
- initiate follow-up
- review prior interactions
- determine whether escalation should increase

---

## Severity 3 — Moderate

Examples:
- unresolved practical need
- repeated follow-up gaps
- volunteer concern
- community conflict concern

### Required Response

```text
Review during operational cycle
```

### Recommended Actions

- assign owner
- monitor continuity
- document follow-up activity

---

# Escalation Ownership Model

Every escalation must have:

- visible ownership
- leadership awareness
- follow-up continuity
- resolution tracking

Escalations must NEVER:
- remain ownerless
- disappear silently
- become hidden without review

---

# Authorized Roles

## Volunteers

Volunteers may:
- acknowledge requests
- initiate follow-up
- flag escalation concerns
- notify leadership

Volunteers should NOT:
- independently manage critical crises
- promise confidentiality beyond policy limits
- attempt clinical counseling
- suppress escalation visibility

---

## Pastors / Leaders

Leaders may:
- review escalations
- assign ownership
- restrict visibility
- coordinate response
- resolve escalations

Leaders are responsible for:
- final escalation decisions
- operational accountability
- continuity oversight

---

## Administrators

Administrators may:
- review operational queues
- support workflow continuity
- monitor unresolved escalations
- assist leadership coordination

Administrators should avoid:
- unnecessary visibility exposure
- operational overreach
- replacing pastoral discernment

---

# Escalation Workflow

## Step 1 — Escalation Trigger

An escalation may be triggered by:
- member request
- volunteer concern
- leader concern
- repeated unresolved distress
- safety concern

---

## Step 2 — Escalation Record Creation

The system should:
- create escalation event
- timestamp escalation
- assign severity level
- restrict visibility if necessary

---

## Step 3 — Leadership Notification

The system should notify:
- designated pastor
- designated escalation leader
- authorized operational contacts

Notification should avoid exposing excessive sensitive detail.

---

## Step 4 — Human Review

Leadership should:
- review context
- determine urgency
- determine next actions
- assign ownership
- document follow-up plan

---

## Step 5 — Follow-Up Continuity

Escalations should remain visible until:
- resolved
- reassigned
- transferred appropriately
- operationally closed

---

# Emergency Services Guidance

The MVP must clearly communicate:

> Dorea is not a replacement for emergency services, crisis hotlines, law enforcement, or licensed counseling.

Leaders should contact:
- emergency services
- crisis hotlines
- appropriate professionals

when immediate danger exists.

---

# Confidentiality Guidelines

The MVP should prioritize:
- respectful handling
- restricted visibility
- limited exposure
- need-to-know access

However:

Confidentiality is NOT absolute when:
- immediate danger exists
- abuse concerns arise
- legal obligations apply
- emergency escalation is required

---

# Moderation Guidelines

## Content Requiring Review

Examples:
- threats
- harassment
- abuse disclosures
- manipulation concerns
- predatory behavior
- repeated unsafe conduct

---

## Moderation Principles

Moderation should:
- prioritize safety
- minimize public exposure
- avoid unnecessary punishment
- preserve dignity whenever possible

---

## Avoid

The MVP should avoid:
- public shaming
- algorithmic suppression
- hidden moderation systems
- opaque enforcement logic
- engagement-driven moderation

---

# Volunteer Protection Guidelines

Volunteers should:
- know escalation boundaries
- know when to involve leadership
- avoid carrying emotional burden alone
- avoid acting beyond competency

Leadership should monitor:
- volunteer fatigue
- emotional overload
- unresolved stress accumulation

---

# Escalation Audit Requirements

The MVP should minimally record:

- escalation timestamp
- triggering user
- assigned owner
- severity level
- follow-up actions
- resolution status

The purpose is:
- continuity
- accountability
- operational visibility

NOT:
- surveillance
- scoring
- behavioral profiling

---

# Escalation Visibility Rules

## Restricted Escalations

Critical escalations should be visible only to:
- authorized leaders
- assigned owners
- authorized administrators

---

## Community Visibility

Escalations should NEVER become publicly visible to the broader community.

---

# Common Operational Scenarios

## Scenario — Self-Harm Concern

Recommended Actions:
- escalate immediately
- notify leadership
- initiate direct human contact
- contact emergency services if appropriate
- document actions taken

---

## Scenario — Abuse Disclosure

Recommended Actions:
- restrict visibility
- notify authorized leaders
- follow legal reporting obligations where applicable
- avoid broad exposure

---

## Scenario — Volunteer Burnout

Recommended Actions:
- redistribute assignments
- reduce workload
- involve leadership
- maintain sustainability focus

---

## Scenario — Harassment Complaint

Recommended Actions:
- review reported interactions
- restrict visibility if necessary
- involve leadership
- document actions taken

---

# Operational Review Cadence

Recommended review frequency:

```text
Weekly during pilot
```

Review topics:
- unresolved escalations
- response delays
- volunteer load
- moderation events
- operational friction
- safety concerns

---

# Deferred Moderation Capabilities

The MVP intentionally avoids:
- automated behavioral scoring
- AI-driven moderation decisions
- predictive risk profiling
- cross-community moderation federation
- advanced trust & safety tooling
- large-scale moderation infrastructure

These are post-MVP concerns.

---

# Moderation Failure Conditions

The moderation system is failing if:
- escalations disappear silently
- leaders are not notified
- volunteers carry crises alone
- visibility controls fail
- unresolved critical concerns persist
- operational burden becomes unsustainable

---

# Final Moderation Principle

Every moderation or escalation decision should answer:

> Does this improve safety, continuity, and human accountability without overwhelming the people trying to help?

If not:
- simplify it
- defer it
- or remove it from MVP operations.
