# PILOT_ONBOARDING_CHECKLIST.md

# Dorea Network — MVP Pilot Onboarding Checklist

## Purpose

This document defines the recommended onboarding process for a Dorea MVP pilot church.

The onboarding process should remain:
- simple
- lightweight
- non-technical
- operationally practical

The goal is NOT to:
- deploy a complex platform
- configure advanced governance systems
- implement enterprise operational controls

The goal is to help a church quickly validate the core Dorea mission:

> No one is left alone.

---

# Pilot Onboarding Philosophy

The onboarding experience should feel:

- approachable
- calm
- relational
- trustworthy
- lightweight

The onboarding process should NOT feel:
- corporate
- highly technical
- administratively overwhelming
- governance-heavy

---

# Recommended Pilot Structure

## Recommended Pilot Size

```text
1–2 churches
50–300 participating members
5–20 volunteers/leaders
```

---

# Recommended Pilot Roles

## Minimum Recommended Staffing

### 1. Pilot Sponsor

Typically:
- pastor
- ministry lead
- church operations leader

Responsibilities:
- approves pilot participation
- defines escalation leadership
- reviews pilot outcomes

---

### 2. Pilot Administrator

Responsibilities:
- volunteer setup
- onboarding coordination
- operational monitoring
- unresolved request review

Recommended:
```text
1–2 people
```

---

### 3. Volunteers

Responsibilities:
- acknowledgment
- follow-up
- support coordination
- escalation support

# Phase 1 — Leadership Orientation

## Objective

Ensure leadership understands:
- mission
- pilot boundaries
- operational expectations
- privacy limitations
- escalation responsibilities

---

## Leadership Orientation Checklist

### Mission Review

- [ ] Review Dorea mission:
  > “No one is left alone.”

- [ ] Review core MVP loop:
  ```text
  Request → Acknowledge → Assign → Follow-Up → Resolve
  ```

# Pilot Communication Guidance

The pilot should be communicated as:

- a continuity support system
- a ministry care coordination tool
- a lightweight support network

The pilot should NOT be positioned as:
- AI ministry replacement
- church social media
- autonomous pastoral system
- advanced analytics platform

---

# Recommended Initial Launch Sequence

## Week 1

- leadership onboarding
- volunteer onboarding
- technical setup
- test workflows

---

## Week 2

- small member rollout
- soft launch
- operational observation

---

## Weeks 3–4

- stabilize workflows
- review unresolved requests
- adjust volunteer load
- collect feedback

---

# Common Pilot Risks

## Risk — Volunteer Overload

Mitigation:
- keep request volume manageable
- rotate ownership
- review workloads weekly

---

## Risk — Operational Complexity

Mitigation:
- keep workflows simple
- defer advanced features
- avoid excessive configuration

---

## Risk — Privacy Confusion

Mitigation:
- clearly explain visibility levels
- explain escalation boundaries
- reinforce confidentiality expectations

---

## Risk — Scope Creep

Mitigation:
- enforce MVP scope
- defer non-essential features
- maintain 4-moment boundary

---

# Pilot Success Indicators

The onboarding process is successful if:

- volunteers understand responsibilities
- pastors trust escalation handling
- members can request support easily
- mobile workflows feel simple
- operational burden remains manageable

---

# Deferred Onboarding Areas

The MVP intentionally defers onboarding for:

- federation setup
- advanced governance configuration
- multi-community orchestration
- replay engine administration
- advanced analytics tooling
- agent ecosystem management

These are post-MVP concerns.

---

# Final Onboarding Principle

Every onboarding decision should answer:

> Does this help the church reliably care for people without overwhelming the people trying to help?

If not:
- simplify it
- defer it
- or remove it from the pilot.
