# SLA_DEFINITIONS.md

# Dorea Network — MVP Service Level Definitions

## Purpose

This document defines the operational service level expectations for the Dorea MVP pilot.

These SLAs are intentionally lightweight.

They exist to support:
- continuity
- accountability
- acknowledgment reliability
- human follow-through
- operational clarity

The MVP SLAs are NOT enterprise operational guarantees.

They are pilot operational targets intended to help ensure:

> No one is left alone.

---

# SLA Philosophy

The Dorea MVP prioritizes:
- human responsiveness
- continuity visibility
- emotional trust
- operational sustainability

The SLAs should:
- encourage reliability
- surface operational gaps
- improve accountability
- remain sustainable for volunteers and pastors

The SLAs should NOT:
- create burnout
- become punitive
- create corporate-style pressure systems
- optimize for performance metrics over human care

---

# Operational Principles

The MVP assumes:

- volunteers are human
- pastors have limited capacity
- churches vary operationally
- emotional situations are unpredictable

The purpose of SLAs is:
- visibility
- consistency
- reliability

NOT:
- perfection
- rigid enforcement
- productivity optimization

---

# SLA Definitions

## SLA-1 — Request Acknowledgment

Every request should receive acknowledgment quickly.

---

### Target

```text
< 60 seconds
```

---

### Applies To

- prayer requests
- emotional support requests
- practical needs
- escalation submissions

---

### Acceptable Acknowledgment Types

- automated acknowledgment
- human acknowledgment
- both

---

### Purpose

The purpose is to ensure members immediately know:

> “Your request was received. You are not alone.”

---

### Failure Conditions

- requests remain unacknowledged
- acknowledgment failures occur silently
- users are uncertain whether requests were received

---

# SLA-2 — Human Visibility

Every request must become visible to at least one responsible human.

---

### Target

```text
Immediate queue visibility
```

---

### Applies To

All submitted requests.

---

### Purpose

The purpose is to prevent:
- orphaned requests
- invisible requests
- silent failures

---

### Failure Conditions

- requests disappear from operational queues
- no accountable owner exists
- unresolved requests become hidden

---

# SLA-3 — Human Assignment

Every active request should receive human ownership.

---

### Target

```text
< 4 hours
```

during normal operational periods.

---

### Applies To

- emotional support
- practical needs
- unresolved prayer requests
- escalation review

---

### Purpose

The purpose is to create:
- accountability
- continuity
- human follow-through

---

### Failure Conditions

- prolonged unassigned requests
- unclear ownership
- assignment ambiguity

---

# SLA-4 — Escalation Review

Urgent concerns require rapid human review.

---

### Target

```text
< 15 minutes
```

for active escalation events.

---

### Applies To

Examples:
- self-harm concerns
- abuse concerns
- immediate safety concerns
- urgent emotional crises

---

### Purpose

The purpose is to ensure:
- leadership visibility
- timely human intervention
- operational awareness

---

### Important Limitation

Dorea is NOT:
- emergency services
- licensed counseling
- law enforcement
- crisis response infrastructure

The MVP exists to:
- surface concerns
- improve continuity
- support human response

---

### Failure Conditions

- escalations remain unseen
- leaders are not notified
- restricted visibility fails
- escalation ownership unclear

---

# SLA-5 — Follow-Up Continuity

Requests should receive ongoing visible follow-up until resolution.

---

### Target

```text
At least one visible follow-up every 24 hours
```

for active unresolved requests.

---

### Applies To

- emotional support
- practical needs
- unresolved escalations
- long-running support situations

---

### Purpose

The purpose is to ensure:
- continuity
- visibility
- relational support
- unresolved awareness

---

### Failure Conditions

- requests stagnate invisibly
- follow-up disappears
- continuity breaks down

---

# SLA-6 — Unresolved Request Visibility

Unresolved requests must remain operationally visible.

---

### Target

```text
Continuous visibility until resolution
```

---

### Purpose

The purpose is to prevent:
- forgotten members
- hidden unresolved needs
- silent operational failures

---

### Failure Conditions

- requests silently expire
- unresolved requests disappear
- no aging visibility exists

---

# SLA-7 — Mobile Responsiveness

Core workflows must remain usable from mobile devices.

---

### Target

Critical workflows should remain functional on:
- iOS mobile devices
- Android mobile devices
- standard mobile browsers

---

### Critical Workflows

- submit request
- view acknowledgment
- follow-up
- assignment visibility
- escalation visibility
- notifications

---

### Purpose

The purpose is to support:
- low friction engagement
- real-world accessibility
- natural daily usage patterns

---

### Failure Conditions

- desktop-only dependency
- poor mobile usability
- excessive navigation complexity

---

# SLA-8 — Operational Sustainability

The MVP must remain sustainable for volunteers and leaders.

---

### Operational Targets

The MVP should:
- minimize administrative burden
- minimize training complexity
- minimize moderation overhead

---

### Purpose

The purpose is to ensure:
- churches can realistically operate the system
- volunteers remain engaged
- pastors do not become overloaded

---

### Failure Conditions

- volunteer burnout
- excessive operational complexity
- heavy governance burden
- unsustainable moderation load

---

# SLA Severity Levels

## Severity 1 — Critical

Examples:
- escalation visibility failure
- authentication failure
- unresolved requests disappearing
- system-wide outage

Expected Response:
```text
Immediate investigation
```

---

## Severity 2 — High

Examples:
- delayed acknowledgments
- assignment failures
- notification delivery issues

Expected Response:
```text
Same-day review
```

---

## Severity 3 — Moderate

Examples:
- mobile usability issue
- dashboard display issue
- delayed follow-up reminders

Expected Response:
```text
Next operational review cycle
```

---

# Pilot SLA Governance

SLAs exist to support:
- operational awareness
- accountability
- continuity

They should NOT:
- punish volunteers
- create guilt-based participation
- optimize for productivity metrics

Human compassion remains more important than numerical compliance.

---

# Deferred SLA Areas

The MVP intentionally avoids SLAs for:
- federation synchronization
- replay engine performance
- analytics latency
- AI recommendation quality
- orchestration scaling
- multi-community routing
- advanced telemetry throughput

These are post-MVP concerns.

---

# SLA Review Cadence

Recommended review frequency:

```text
Weekly during pilot
```

Review should focus on:
- acknowledgment reliability
- unresolved requests
- escalation handling
- volunteer sustainability
- operational friction

---

# Final SLA Principle

Every SLA should answer:

> Does this improve continuity, accountability, and emotional trust without overwhelming the people trying to help?

If not:
- simplify it
- defer it
- or remove it from MVP operations.
