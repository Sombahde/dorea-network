# PILOT_OPERATIONS.md

# Dorea Network — MVP Pilot Operations

## Purpose

This document defines the operational model for the Dorea MVP pilot.

The pilot exists to validate the core Dorea mission:

> No one is left alone.

The pilot is NOT intended to:
- prove scalability
- prove federation
- prove advanced runtime orchestration
- validate enterprise architecture
- optimize engagement metrics

The pilot exists to determine whether Dorea meaningfully improves:
- acknowledgment
- follow-through
- visibility of unresolved needs
- emotional support continuity
- ministry coordination

within a real church environment.

---

# Pilot Philosophy

The pilot should optimize for:

- simplicity
- operational learning
- emotional usefulness
- trust
- sustainability
- low friction
- human-centered interaction

The pilot should avoid:
- excessive administration
- heavy governance complexity
- feature overload
- workflow bureaucracy
- technical sophistication for its own sake

---

# Pilot Scope

## Initial Pilot Size

The MVP pilot should remain intentionally constrained.

Recommended scope:

```text
1–2 churches
50–300 participating members
5–20 volunteers/leaders
```

The pilot should remain:
- manageable
- observable
- adaptable
- operationally lightweight

---

# Pilot Duration

Recommended initial pilot duration:

```text
60–90 days
```

This allows evaluation of:
- adoption behavior
- follow-through consistency
- volunteer sustainability
- pastoral trust
- unresolved request handling

---

# Pilot Goals

The pilot should validate:

1. Requests are acknowledged reliably
2. Requests receive human ownership
3. Follow-up continuity improves
4. Unresolved needs remain visible
5. Volunteers can sustain participation
6. Pastors trust the process
7. Members feel less isolated
8. The operational burden remains manageable

---

# Pilot Roles

## 1. Pilot Sponsor

Typically:
- pastor
- ministry leader
- church operations leader

Responsibilities:
- approve pilot participation
- define leadership contacts
- review pilot outcomes
- oversee escalation handling

---

## 2. Pilot Administrator

Responsible for:
- onboarding volunteers
- monitoring unresolved requests
- reviewing escalation queues
- weekly operational review

Recommended:
```text
1–2 people maximum
```

---

## 3. Volunteers

Responsibilities:
- acknowledgment
- follow-up
- prayer support
- practical coordination

Volunteers should:
- receive lightweight training
- understand confidentiality expectations
- know escalation boundaries

---

## 4. Members

Members may:
- request support
- receive follow-up
- participate in encouragement
- support others when appropriate

---

# MVP Operational Rules

## Rule 1 — Every Request Must Be Acknowledged

No request should remain unacknowledged.

Target:
```text
Acknowledgment within 60 seconds
```

Acknowledgment may be:
- automated
- human
- both

---

## Rule 2 — Every Request Must Have Human Visibility

Every request must become visible to at least one responsible human.

The MVP does NOT support:
- fully autonomous handling
- invisible routing
- unresolved hidden requests

---

## Rule 3 — Requests Cannot Silently Expire

Requests must remain visible until:
- resolved
- reassigned
- escalated
- intentionally closed

---

## Rule 4 — Escalations Require Human Oversight

Urgent concerns require:
- designated leadership visibility
- restricted access
- human discernment

The MVP does NOT replace:
- emergency services
- licensed counseling
- law enforcement
- crisis intervention professionals

---

# Pilot Onboarding Process

## Church Onboarding

### Step 1 — Leadership Orientation

Review:
- mission
- pilot expectations
- operational boundaries
- escalation limitations
- privacy expectations

Estimated time:
```text
30–60 minutes
```

---

### Step 2 — Volunteer Setup

Volunteers receive:
- basic training
- role explanation
- escalation guidelines
- confidentiality expectations

Estimated time:
```text
30 minutes
```

---

### Step 3 — Member Invitation

Members join through:
- QR code
- invite link
- church communication

The onboarding flow should:
- complete in under 3 minutes
- require minimal setup
- avoid technical complexity

---

# Volunteer Operational Expectations

Volunteers are expected to:

- acknowledge requests
- follow up consistently
- escalate appropriately
- maintain confidentiality
- communicate compassionately

Volunteers are NOT expected to:
- provide licensed counseling
- resolve all practical needs
- remain continuously available
- replace pastoral leadership

---

# Escalation Operations

## Escalation Examples

Examples include:
- self-harm concern
- abuse concern
- immediate safety concern
- urgent emotional crisis

---

## Escalation Flow

1. Request flagged
2. Authorized leaders notified
3. Escalation reviewed by human
4. Appropriate response initiated
5. Follow-up documented

---

## Escalation Restrictions

Escalation data should:
- remain restricted
- minimize visibility
- avoid unnecessary exposure

---

# Weekly Operational Review

Recommended cadence:
```text
Weekly
```

Review topics:

- unresolved requests
- aging requests
- escalation events
- volunteer load
- response delays
- member feedback
- operational friction

# Incident Handling

The pilot must define clear response expectations for:

- safety concerns
- abuse concerns
- suicidal ideation
- harassment
- confidentiality violations

The MVP must maintain:
- documented escalation paths
- leader visibility
- restricted access

---

# Privacy Expectations

The pilot should:
- minimize collected data
- avoid unnecessary exposure
- maintain confidentiality
- respect emotional sensitivity

The MVP intentionally avoids:
- behavioral profiling
- engagement scoring
- psychological analytics
- advertising-oriented data collection

---

# Pilot Success Criteria

The pilot is considered successful if:

- requests are reliably acknowledged
- unresolved needs remain visible
- follow-up improves meaningfully
- volunteers remain sustainable
- pastors trust the workflow
- members report feeling supported
- operational overhead remains manageable

---

# Deferred Pilot Capabilities

The pilot intentionally defers:

- federation between churches
- advanced replay systems
- deep telemetry analytics
- AI-driven recommendation systems
- adaptive governance systems
- generalized agent ecosystems
- advanced social mechanics
- large-scale optimization architecture

---

# Pilot Exit Criteria

At the conclusion of the pilot:

Leadership should evaluate:

1. Did Dorea improve care continuity?
2. Did fewer requests fall through cracks?
3. Did members feel less isolated?
4. Was the operational burden sustainable?
5. Did pastors trust the workflow?
6. Should the pilot continue, expand, or pause?

---

# Final Operational Principle

Every operational decision should answer:

> Does this help ensure that no one is left alone without overwhelming the people trying to help?

If not:
- simplify it
- defer it
- or remove it from MVP operations.
