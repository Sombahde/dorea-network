# PRIVACY_AND_SAFETY_MVP.md

# Dorea Network — MVP Privacy & Safety Guidelines

## Purpose

This document defines the minimum privacy, confidentiality, and safety expectations for the Dorea MVP.

The Dorea MVP exists to support people experiencing:
- struggle
- loneliness
- discouragement
- emotional distress
- practical need
- crisis situations

Because Dorea may involve emotionally sensitive interactions, privacy and safety are foundational operational requirements.

The MVP should prioritize:
- trust
- emotional safety
- confidentiality
- minimal data collection
- responsible escalation
- human accountability

---

# Core Principle

> People must feel safe asking for help.

The MVP should reduce fear, hesitation, and isolation — not create additional emotional risk.

---

# Privacy Philosophy

The MVP follows a principle of:

> Collect minimally. Expose minimally. Retain intentionally.

The platform should avoid:
- unnecessary personal data
- behavioral profiling
- engagement scoring
- psychological analysis
- advertising-oriented tracking
- surveillance-oriented telemetry

The MVP exists to support continuity and care, not maximize engagement.

---

# Human-Centered Safety Philosophy

The MVP prioritizes:
- human discernment
- pastoral oversight
- volunteer accountability
- trusted relationships

AI may assist with:
- reminders
- acknowledgment
- visibility
- lightweight routing

AI must NOT:
- provide therapy
- make crisis determinations independently
- replace human escalation
- impersonate trusted relationships
- make autonomous safety decisions

---

# Minimum Data Collection

The MVP should collect only what is necessary to support:
- acknowledgment
- follow-up
- assignment
- escalation
- continuity

Recommended minimum member data:

```text
First name
Last name
Email
Optional phone number
Role
Notification preferences
```

The MVP should avoid:
- demographic enrichment
- social graph collection
- behavioral analytics
- unnecessary profile expansion

---

# Sensitive Request Handling

Sensitive requests may include:
- emotional distress
- marital issues
- financial hardship
- abuse concerns
- self-harm concerns
- family crises

These requests require:
- restricted visibility
- limited access
- careful handling
- intentional follow-up

---

# Visibility Levels

The MVP supports only simple visibility levels:

```text
private
leaders
community
```

---

## Private

Visible only to:
- assigned leaders
- assigned volunteers
- authorized administrators

---

## Leaders

Visible only to:
- pastors
- approved leaders
- assigned volunteers

---

## Community

Visible to:
- approved community members
- according to community governance rules

---

# Access Control Principles

The MVP should implement:
- role-based access
- authenticated sessions
- restricted escalation visibility
- least-privilege access

The MVP should avoid:
- broad unrestricted visibility
- excessive administrative access
- hidden shadow moderation systems

---

# Escalation Safety Rules

## Human Escalation Required

All escalation events require human review.

The MVP must NOT:
- autonomously intervene
- provide emergency services
- claim clinical authority
- replace licensed professionals

---

## Escalation Examples

Examples include:
- self-harm concern
- abuse concern
- immediate safety risk
- severe emotional crisis

---

## Escalation Workflow

1. Concern submitted
2. Escalation event created
3. Authorized leaders notified
4. Human review occurs
5. Appropriate next steps determined
6. Follow-up documented

---

# Emergency Disclaimer

The MVP must clearly communicate:

> Dorea is not a replacement for emergency services, licensed counseling, or crisis hotlines.

The platform should instruct users to contact:
- emergency services
- licensed professionals
- crisis hotlines

when immediate danger exists.

---

# Volunteer Safety Expectations

Volunteers should:
- receive lightweight safety orientation
- understand confidentiality expectations
- understand escalation boundaries
- know when to involve leadership

Volunteers should NOT:
- attempt to act as therapists
- handle severe crises independently
- promise confidentiality beyond policy boundaries
- become sole responders for high-risk situations

---

# Confidentiality Principles

The MVP should emphasize:
- respectful handling of requests
- limited sharing
- intentional access boundaries

However:

The MVP should also communicate clearly that:
- escalations may require leadership involvement
- safety concerns may require broader intervention
- confidentiality is not absolute in emergency situations

---

# Audit & Accountability

The MVP should maintain lightweight audit visibility for:
- request acknowledgment
- assignment changes
- escalation events
- resolution actions

The purpose is:
- accountability
- continuity
- operational visibility

NOT:
- surveillance
- behavioral scoring
- volunteer ranking

---

# Notification Safety

Notifications should:
- minimize sensitive detail exposure
- avoid displaying private information on lock screens
- respect visibility boundaries

Examples:

Preferred:
```text
You have a new support request.
```

Avoid:
```text
John is depressed and needs help immediately.
```

---

# Data Retention Philosophy

The MVP should retain data only as long as operationally necessary.

Retention principles:
- minimize storage duration
- archive intentionally
- avoid unnecessary replication
- avoid indefinite retention by default

The MVP intentionally avoids:
- telemetry warehousing
- behavioral history systems
- engagement profiling databases

---

# Security Expectations

Minimum MVP security expectations include:

- authenticated access
- encrypted transport (HTTPS)
- secure password handling
- role-based permissions
- restricted escalation visibility
- secure cloud hosting practices

The MVP does NOT require:
- enterprise-grade federation security
- advanced zero-trust architecture
- large-scale SIEM integrations
- complex policy engines

Those are deferred beyond MVP.

---

# Moderator / Leader Responsibilities

Leaders should:
- review unresolved escalations
- monitor volunteer sustainability
- intervene when safety concerns arise
- maintain confidentiality expectations

The MVP should avoid:
- hidden moderation
- algorithmic suppression
- opaque intervention systems

---

# Anonymous Encouragement Feed Safety

If the MVP includes anonymous encouragement:

The feed should avoid:
- popularity mechanics
- follower systems
- performance incentives
- engagement ranking
- social pressure dynamics

The feed exists to:
- encourage
- comfort
- reinforce hope
- support continuity

NOT:
- maximize activity
- create influencers
- optimize retention metrics

---

# Pilot Privacy Expectations

Pilot churches should:
- communicate privacy expectations clearly
- define escalation contacts
- explain visibility levels
- explain confidentiality boundaries
- define volunteer responsibilities

Members should understand:
- who may see requests
- when leaders may intervene
- how escalations are handled

---

# Safety Incident Categories

The MVP should define response paths for:

- self-harm concern
- abuse concern
- harassment
- volunteer misconduct
- confidentiality breach
- threatening behavior

The MVP should maintain:
- documented escalation paths
- leadership accountability
- restricted access handling

---

# Deferred Privacy & Safety Features

The following are intentionally deferred beyond MVP:

- advanced behavioral monitoring
- AI-driven risk prediction
- automated crisis classification
- deep analytics pipelines
- cross-community moderation
- federation governance systems
- advanced compliance frameworks
- large-scale trust & safety tooling

---

# Final Privacy & Safety Principle

Every privacy or safety decision should answer:

> Does this increase trust, emotional safety, and human accountability without creating unnecessary surveillance or operational burden?

If not:
- simplify it
- defer it
- or remove it from MVP scope.
