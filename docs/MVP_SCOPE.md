# Dorea Network — MVP Scope Definition

## Purpose

This document defines the execution boundaries for the initial Dorea MVP.

The Dorea repository contains long-range architectural doctrine, governance models, continuity systems, runtime concepts, telemetry patterns, and future platform capabilities.

Those documents remain intentional and valuable.

However:

The MVP exists to validate the core emotional and operational hypothesis of Dorea before broader platform expansion.

---

# Core Mission

> No one is left alone.

Dorea exists to help communities ensure that people experiencing struggle, isolation, discouragement, crisis, or practical need are acknowledged, connected, and followed up with through intentional human support.

The MVP is designed to prove this mission in the simplest possible operational form.

---

# MVP Goal

The MVP must validate the following hypothesis:

> A mobile-first continuity and care coordination system can help churches respond more consistently and compassionately to people before isolation becomes crisis.

Success is NOT measured by architectural sophistication.

Success IS measured by:

- acknowledgment reliability
- follow-through consistency
- emotional trust
- pastoral usefulness
- volunteer sustainability
- perceived community support

---

# MVP Identity

The MVP should behave primarily as:

- a ministry care coordination platform
- a continuity and follow-up system
- a mobile-first support network
- a human-centered acknowledgment engine

The MVP is NOT attempting to become:

- a generalized AI platform
- a federated governance ecosystem
- a social media network
- a broad autonomous agent framework
- a complex simulation platform
- a full replay analytics system

Those capabilities may emerge later.

They are NOT MVP requirements.

---

# Core MVP Loop

The MVP must reliably support the following loop:

1. A member submits a need, request, concern, or prayer.
2. The request is acknowledged immediately.
3. A trusted human is assigned ownership.
4. Follow-up reminders continue until resolved.
5. Unresolved requests remain visible.
6. No request disappears silently.

This loop is the foundation of the platform.

---

# Initial Supported Moments

The MVP will initially support only a minimal set of moments:

1. Prayer Request
2. Emotional Support
3. Practical Need
4. Escalation / Urgent Concern

Additional moments are intentionally deferred.

---

# Human-Centered Operational Rule

The MVP optimizes for:

> Human response reliability over AI sophistication.

AI may assist:

- routing
- reminders
- acknowledgment
- continuity
- visibility

AI must NOT replace:

- discernment
- pastoral care
- human compassion
- trusted relationships
- accountability

Human ownership remains central.

---

# Mobile-First Requirement

The MVP is designed primarily for mobile engagement.

Why:

- people experience crisis in moments, not workflows
- support must exist within natural daily behavior
- friction kills follow-through
- care systems must feel immediate and lightweight

The MVP should prioritize:

- simplicity
- responsiveness
- emotional safety
- low-friction interaction

---

# Governance Scope (MVP)

The MVP governance model should remain intentionally lightweight.

MVP governance includes:

- basic community roles
- acknowledgment ownership
- escalation visibility
- consent boundaries
- delegation controls
- simple audit visibility

The MVP intentionally avoids:

- complex policy combinatorics
- advanced federation governance
- adaptive policy systems
- advanced runtime simulation
- deep replay analysis

---

# Telemetry Scope (MVP)

Telemetry exists only to support:

- request visibility
- acknowledgment tracking
- follow-up continuity
- unresolved request detection
- operational accountability

Telemetry is NOT intended initially for:

- behavioral optimization
- engagement maximization
- surveillance
- advanced predictive modeling

---

# Deferred Capabilities

The following concepts are intentionally deferred until after operational validation:

- federation between communities
- advanced replay engines
- deep telemetry analytics
- policy simulation systems
- broad agent marketplaces
- advanced command center analytics
- adaptive governance systems
- generalized runtime orchestration
- complex multi-surface coordination
- advanced AI-driven recommendation systems

These remain part of the long-range architectural direction but are not required for MVP validation.

---

# MVP Success Criteria

The MVP is successful if pilot communities demonstrate:

- requests are consistently acknowledged
- follow-up improves
- fewer people feel ignored
- pastors trust the workflow
- volunteers remain engaged
- response ownership is visible
- unresolved needs are surfaced clearly
- members feel less alone

---

# Pilot Scope

Initial pilots should remain intentionally constrained:

- 1–2 churches maximum
- lightweight onboarding
- limited governance complexity
- low operational burden
- mobile-first usage
- human-centered workflows
- simple moderation requirements

The purpose of the pilot is learning, not scale.

---

# Architectural Philosophy

The broader Dorea architecture remains intentional.

The repository contains future-oriented concepts including:

- continuity models
- replay systems
- governance doctrine
- telemetry fabrics
- runtime orchestration
- delegated authority structures

These concepts exist to preserve long-range architectural integrity.

However:

The MVP must prove emotional and operational usefulness before broader platform expansion occurs.

---

# Final Principle

Every MVP decision should be evaluated against one question:

> Does this help ensure that no one is left alone?
